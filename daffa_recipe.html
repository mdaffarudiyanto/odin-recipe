Indonesian Yakisoba

What is Indonesian Yakisoba?

Yakisoba is a popular Japanese stir-fried noodle dish that typically includes vegetables and meat, and is seasoned with a sweet and savory sauce. However, in Indonesia, yakisoba has been adapted to suit local tastes and preferences, resulting in a unique version of the dish that is widely enjoyed in the country. In the Indonesian version of yakisoba, the dish is often made with spicy seasonings and a variety of local ingredients such as chili, soy sauce, and kecap manis (a sweet soy sauce). The noodles used in Indonesian yakisoba are typically thin and have a slightly chewy texture, giving the dish a unique mouthfeel that is different from its Japanese counterpart.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of Indomie (or any other Indonesian instant noodle)

How to make

1. Boil the water

Boil 2-3 cups of water in a pot.

2. Add the Indomie

Add the Indomie noodles to the boiling water and let it cook for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

3. Pour out the water

After 3 minutes, remove the pot from the heat and pour out the excess water.

4. Add the Indomie seasoning packets.

Add the Indomie seasoning packets (flavoring powder and chili powder) and stir well

5. Enjoy!

Enjoy your delicious Indonesian Yakisoba!
